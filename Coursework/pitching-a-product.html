PITCHING A PRODUCT

This page provides a step-by-step journey of how I developed the idea, refined it, and created a successful pitch for my product.

Introduction & Client Brief

Client Name: SensorIT

Project Name: Vulnerable People Safety Device

SensorIT is an innovative company specialising in Internet of Things (IoT) solutions.

This project focuses on the design and promotion of a GPS-enabled, battery-powered safety device targeted at vulnerable individuals.

The device is fully cellular-connected, allowing users to seek help without reliance on mobile apps, passwords, or unlocking their phones.

The key component of the product is its simplicity and discreet functionality, designed to provide peace of mind and immediate assistance.

This brief outlines the development of a pitch to showcase the device's design, branding, unique features, and promotional strategies.

Step 1: Ideation and Inspiration

Every great product begins with an idea.

I started by comparing existing products on the markets. With the existing products, I focus on the problems people face daily and how I could solve them creatively.

This stage involved viewing the competitors and filling in what was lacking. Sometimes all it takes is to reinvent the wheel (no pun intended later on).

Step 2: Refining the Vision

Once the core idea and the competition were identified, I began refining it through detailed brainstorming and visualisation.

Using tools like mood boards and images, I transformed abstract concepts into clear, focused ideas that aligned with my goals.

With using items from the internet, I have thought to keep my concept technological.

I have also included a moodboard for how the eventual advertising concept scene was to become.

Step 3: Preparing the Pitch

The next step was crafting the pitch. This involved creating a compelling narrative that showcased the product's value, supported by visuals and data. I paid attention to the flow and impact, ensuring my presentation resonated with the audience.

Step 4: Delivering the Pitch

Finally, it was time to deliver the pitch. This video showcases the complete presentation, incorporating all the elements developed in previous steps.

Conclusion

Pitching a product is no easy task.

With constant refinement of ideas and practicalities, it all comes down to how you market the product.

With the Vulnerable People Safety Device, what mattered the most was creating something that was actually going to be useful and revolutionary rather than useless and ordinary.

The feedback on my presentation was sufficient. I have improved the presentation after that.
